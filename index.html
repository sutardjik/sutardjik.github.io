Karen Sutardji is a developer and designer crafting engaging experiences for human betterment.

Featured Works

Spiraling ↗

A 2D generative art visualizer that meditates on the cyclical turbulence of early 20s self-sabotage—a common experience marked by existential questioning and internal turmoil. Likened to the ephemeral nature of wave ripples, problems within the self gradually multiply until reaching a “breaking point.” The cycle repeats as ebbs and flows starting at the core from within and expand outward to reflect the emergence of external challenges. Concrete poetic elements are used within the spatial arrangement and visual dynamics of text to enhance the thematic exploration of internal and external struggles.

Switchblade ↗

A self-written poem as an introductory dive into creative writing that is generated through animation using the Typed.js library. The typing cursor style subtly echoes that of ChatGPT, while the color scheme switches from light to dark mode after the completion of the stanzas. The narrated events reflect real-life experiences, adding depth and intrigue to the poem’s narrative.

.calm ↗

A personal aesthetics gallery that draws inspiration from the minimalist styles of VSCO and the visual appeal of Pinterest to challenge the toxic nature of conventional social media platforms by advocating for the slow web movement. This digital space is meant for exploring and appreciating curated aesthetics, encouraging mindful interaction and emphasizing quality over quantity in the digital realm.

Ascension ↗

An interactive lyric visualizer—a creative coding website—that showcases the impact of diction in song lyrics through varied typography choices. It is centered around the lyrics of the vocal piano ballad “Ascension” by Sarah Kinsley. A featured interactive still-life sakura garden showcases carefully chosen emojis to enhance the symbolism, emphasizing the deeper meaning of the song.

QuasiRealSync ↗

A data replication tool that seamlessly captures and parses binary logs from MySQL, facilitating efficient transfer to target PostgreSQL databases for replication. This tool supports CRUD operations and ensures a smooth and reliable replication process, guaranteeing data integrity while minimizing downtime.

PanPop ↗

An interactive population trends visualizer of six countries from 1950 to 2020 through population pyramids and annual population growth line graphs to explore population data and understand demographic changes over time. Interactive sliders can be manipulated to view data for specific years, with data sources linked to PopulationPyramid.net for additional information and context.




Welcome to my little corner of the internet!

I’m Karen, a bicontinental developer and self-taught designer based in Jakarta and San Francisco.

I hold a B.E. in Computer Science from South China University of Technology. My practice is dedicated to embedding creativity, accessibility, and functionality in digital spaces. Simultaneously, I focus on crafting art that embodies curiosity, potential, and personal connection, bridging the relationship between the curated digital self and the personal self. I aspire to create a more conscious digital world, driven by my interest in ethical technology and its intersection with human behavior.

When I’m not doing that, you can find me visiting art museums, delving into ethereal indie pop, or going on solitary nature walks.
